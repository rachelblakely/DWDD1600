Who Were the Signers?

Declaration of Independence, adopted on July 4, 1776, was signed by 56 delegates to the Second Continental Congress, representing the 13 American colonies. While all signers played important roles, some were especially prominent:

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress. Famous for his large and bold signature, symbolizing American defiance. Represented Massachusetts.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Represented Virginia. Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Elder statesman and diplomat from Pennsylvania. Helped gain French support during the Revolution. A key figure in American independence.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence from Massachusetts. Helped draft the Declaration. Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Roger Sherman
Delegate from Connecticut. Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents. Member of the committee to draft the Declaration.
Robert R. Livingston
Delegate from New York. Member of the Committee of Five to draft the Declaration, but did not sign the final document.
